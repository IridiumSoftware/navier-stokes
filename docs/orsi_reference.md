## Core axiom

> **Constraint licenses regime. Regime licenses truth. Truth does not license constraint.**

The arrow is one-way: **Constraint → Regime → Truth**. You cannot reverse-engineer constraints from
accepted truths; **truth is regime-local** (not a global license). Operating principle: *constraint-
origin first*. Sovereign output: the **MDAGC — Minimal Decontaminated Admissible Generator Class**.
Objective: *recover the admissible generator class by governing validity, ontology, problem formation,
discovery, and formalism at the highest level.*

## The 10 top-level components (each a governance interface)

1. **Validity-World Formation** — conditions for admissibility worlds; how validity domains arise; the
   boundary of what can count; pre-epistemic ordering; formation of evaluable reality.
2. **Admissibility Sovereignty** — who/what licenses fundamentals; authority over primitives; legitimate
   transition-claims; override of *predictive prestige*; final admissibility jurisdiction.
3. **Reality-Interface Arbitration** — substrate↔representation mediation; transitions↔observation
   interface; arbitration of measurable access; **prevention of substrate smuggling**; representation
   rights.
4. **Ontology-Licensing Authority** — when concepts become fundamental; primitive-authorization rules;
   distinguish *export* from *ontology*; **ban on premature ontological promotion**; certification of
   irreducible entities.
5. **Problem-Space Origination** — how valid problems form; **detection of smuggled assumptions**;
   reframing contaminated questions; admissible target selection; solution-space boundary design.
6. **Generator-Class Sovereignty** — supremacy of admissible generators; **recovery of minimal generator
   classes**; export equations as *consequences*; substrate-neutral architecture; transition-first core.
7. **Discovery-Validity Constitution** — what makes discovery valid; discovery vs *consensus*; conditions
   for real novelty; verified regime-transition rules; **discovery without laundering**.
8. **Formalism Legitimacy Audit** — audit of equations/formalisms; legitimacy of representation
   structures; **detection of overlicensed mathematics**; **quarantine of export surplus**;
   return-transport accountability.
9. **Truth-Production Governance** — governance of truth-generating systems; selection pressure on
   accepted claims; filtering of false authority; **constraint on consensus manufacture**; production of
   durable validity.
10. **Constraint-Origin Constitution** — where constraints come from; origin of governing limits;
    structure of preformal restrictions; source of lawful exclusions; constitution of possible
    transitions.

## How it relates to CCATT and to this program (observations, not claims)

- **ORSI is the parent governance architecture; CCATT (`docs/ccatt_reference.md`) is its transport
  layer.** ORSI = the *constitution* (what may be admitted, by whom, under what constraint); CCATT
  ("Certified Constraint-Access Transport Theory") = the *mechanism* for moving certified invariants
  across regimes with an explicit loss ledger. Both are constraint-first, decontamination-based, and aim
  at the admissible **generator class** (ORSI's MDAGC; CCATT's "generators explain exports, not vice
  versa"). ORSI §6 (Generator-Class Sovereignty) and §8 (Formalism Legitimacy Audit) are the CCATT
  hooks.
- **This obstruction program's firewall+witness discipline is a concrete *instance* of ORSI §8 + §9.**
  "Detection of overlicensed mathematics / quarantine of export surplus" (§8) is *exactly* what the
  triad witnesses + the probe-first did this arc — the **six over-reaches** were each "export surplus"
  (claims exporting more than the within-truncation constraints license: "exhibits / irreducibly /
  reduces to the wall / blocks the reduction"). "Truth does not license constraint" ≈ the firewall
  ("predictive success / elegance / dominance don't license a primitive"; `:proved`=0 by design). The
  `Scope` tags ≈ ORSI §3 (prevention of substrate smuggling). NS-037's inverse-Born map ≈ ORSI §6
  (recover the generator class; exports are consequences).
