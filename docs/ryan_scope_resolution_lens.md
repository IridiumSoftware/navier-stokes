## §1 — Ryan's framework (the machinery)

He replaces "level" (which conflates two things) with three primitives:
- **Scope S** — the spatial/temporal boundary: *which components* are in the system
  (the extent). Broaden = include more; narrow = fewer.
- **Resolution R** — the finest distinguishable distinction (`H=log n`, Shannon).
  An attribute of the *description*: **epistemic**. (Scale, by contrast, is ontological.)
- **State** — the info distinguishing configurations up to a resolution at a moment.
- Macro M vs micro μ: **R_M ≤ R_μ** (macro coarser), **S_M ≥ S_μ** (macro broader),
  with at least one strict.

The decisive split (Defs 1–4):
- **Class I — weak emergence:** macro & micro differ *only in resolution*. A
  coarse-graining map `C: μ→M` exists; the property is detectable in μ by applying C
  ⇒ **epistemic**, "a limitation of the observer, not a property of the system." Worst
  case C is incompressible ⇒ "the optimal means of prediction is simulation."
- **Class II — novel emergence:** macro & micro differ *in scope*. Present only at a
  **minimal macrostate M\*** (smallest scope exhibiting it), absent in any
  narrower-scope microstate ⇒ at least partly **ontological**. Examples: the Möbius
  strip's Euler characteristic χ (a topological invariant, present only at full scope,
  *resolution-independent*); perfect secret-sharing (D recoverable only at scope ≥ k;
  mutual info `I(D;D′)=0` below k).
- **Thesis:** emergence is coupled to **scope** (novel/ontological), not **resolution**
  (weak/epistemic). Emergence requires *extended* structure; nonlinearity is necessary;
  independent / linear / averaged components cannot emerge.

## §2 — The mapping is near-literal (not analogy)

- Ryan's **resolution R ≈ our grid N / k_max** — the finest scale the description resolves.
- Ryan's **scope S ≈ the spatial domain / a global integral** — the whole flow.

So a **resolution-coupled** NS quantity = one that changes with N (a spectrum-slope
fit); a **scope-coupled** NS quantity = a resolution-invariant global integral (σ=0).
This is almost definitional, which is why the readings below are sharp.
