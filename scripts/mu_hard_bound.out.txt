  • YES — the hard layer bounds μ from above: μ ≤ 1, rigorously, from monotonicity
    (ζ_6 ≥ ζ_3 = 1, holding for any regular flow with bounded velocity). And μ ≥ 0 from
    concavity (ζ_6 ≤ 2). So the frame-independent invariants force μ ∈ [0,1].

    CKN is vacuous for regular flow (no h<0); concavity and D≤3 permit μ all the way to 1.
  • The observed μ≈0.20–0.25 sits in the INTERIOR of [0,1]. The gap from 1 down to ~0.2 is
    exactly the FRAME-DEPENDENT content the anti-anchoring discipline (§9) forbids importing
    as structure — μ's specific value is protected by NO frame-independent invariant.
  • This is the methodology working, not failing: it brackets μ structurally and then HONESTLY
    STOPS at the boundary between what's forced and what's measured. A loose bound that is real
    beats a tight bound that is smuggled. Scope: empirical; :proved=0.
