  VERDICT — both naive mechanisms FAIL; the closure prediction is REFUTED
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  B (measured here) = d(lnκ)/dRe = 0.0151 /Re.
  • NAIVE viscous-decay: REFUTED. κ·Re varies 4× ⇒ κ is exponential in Re,
    while a viscous rate is algebraic (1/Re). Different physics.
  • CLOSURE-CYCLE occupancy (κ ∝ P_qs near threshold): ALSO REFUTED.
    The quasi-stationary weight near the laminar boundary retreats far too
    SLOWLY — it explains only 16–47% of B (across E_esc=0.002…0.008);
    κ/P_qs is not flat (varies ~2–2.6×). The mean quasi-stationary amplitude
    barely moves (⟨E⟩: 0.0548→0.0500) while κ drops 6×.
  ⇒ The exponential τ(Re) is NOT 'occupancy of a low-amplitude region.' Most of
    B lives in the CONDITIONAL dynamics: once the flow dips toward laminar, the
    SSP cycle increasingly RECOVERS at higher Re — escape needs dip AND failure-
    to-recover, and the recovery probability carries the dominant Re-dependence.

  HONEST SCORE for the closure lens:
   – QUALITATIVE (it's a metastable/large-deviation escape ⇒ exponential,
     memoryless): consistent — but this is generic dynamical-systems framing,
     shared with the standard saddle picture, NOT a unique closure win.
   – QUANTITATIVE: the one concrete closure-cycle prediction tested here
     (occupancy-limited escape) FAILED. B remains unpredicted, and the data
     point to the cycle's RECOVERY/commitment probability as where B lives.
  This is the firewall working: a falsifiable closure prediction, mostly refuted.
