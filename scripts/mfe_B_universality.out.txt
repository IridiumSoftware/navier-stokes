  VERDICT — what is, and isn't, universal about B
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  • FORM: τ(Re)≈exp(A+B·Re) holds with R²(lnτ–Re)>0.95 in every case run.
  • AMPLITUDE: B is invariant to seeding (spread 8.0% over A∈[0.1,0.5]).
    ⇒ B is a property of the SADDLE, not of the initial condition. Real.
  • GEOMETRY: B DOES drift with the box (0.00807 … 0.01300, 44% spread).
    ⇒ The exponential FORM is universal; the RATE B = B(α,γ) is geometry-specific.

  SHARPENED PREDICTION BAR: a closure theory must predict the FORM (escape
  rate exponential in Re) AND the FUNCTION B(α,γ) — not a single constant.
  Amplitude-invariance means there IS a well-posed B to predict; geometry-
  dependence means the prediction must carry the box in, as the real saddle does.
