  observe which invariants are scale-COVARIANT (gauge) vs scale-INVARIANT (meaningful).

  state: seeded helical field (kmax=2), N=32.  div-free; H≠0.
    E=0.500000  H=-0.443714  Ω=1.395773  P=4.444978

  (A) ROTATION (90° about z; det=+1) — the COMPACT generator
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    invariant    original       rotated        Δrel      
    E            0.500000       0.500000       1.0e-15

  (B) SCALING u↦λu(λx) — the NON-COMPACT generator (NS-002)

    Subtlety (got this right after a first wrong pass): the mode-shift k→λk on a FIXED
    torus with a FIXED mean gives the FIELD-frame exponents E~λ², H~λ³, Ω~λ⁴, P~λ⁶.
    Verify those are EXACT (ratio Iλ/(λ^p·I₀) → 1):
    λ      clipped  E/(λ²E₀)       H/(λ³H₀)       Ω/(λ⁴Ω₀)       P/(λ⁶P₀)      
    2      0        1.000000       1.000000       1.000000       1.000000      
    3      0        1.000000       1.000000       1.000000       1.000000      
    The NS scaling symmetry ALSO rescales the DOMAIN 𝕋_{2π}→𝕋_{2π/λ} — a λ⁻³ volume
    factor in every integral. PHYSICAL exponent = field exponent − 3:
        E ~ λ⁻¹   (SUPERCRITICAL: negative ⇒ →0 as λ→∞)
        H ~ λ⁰    (SCALE-INVARIANT — the topological Casimir)
        Ω ~ λ⁺¹ ,  P ~ λ⁺³   (scale-COVARIANT)
    ⇒ the supercriticality lives in the DOMAIN/measure scaling, not the amplitude alone —
      you cannot see it from field-scaling on a fixed box. Scale is a genuine coordinate.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  (C) THE SCALING QUOTIENT — physical exponent q of each combination (q=0 ⇒ descends)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    combination      physical q     scale-invariant?
    H                λ^0            ← YES (descends to quotient)
    E·Ω              λ^0            ← YES (descends to quotient)
    E                λ^-1           no (gauge)
    Ω                λ^1            no (gauge)
    E·√Ω             λ^-0.5         no (gauge)
    P/Ω²             λ^1            no (gauge)
    H/√(E·Ω)         λ^0            ← YES (descends to quotient)
    ⇒ H (q=0) and E·Ω (q=−1+1=0) are SCALE-INVARIANT ⇒ the 'meaningful' coordinates of the
      scaling quotient. The scale-invariant quotient is the CRITICAL level — exactly the norm
      class (NS-002/NS-005) where regularity is decided; absolute E,Ω,P are pure gauge there.

  • The symmetry group SPLITS the manifold's invariants two ways:
    – ROTATION (compact): E,Ω,P,H are scalars/pseudoscalar ⇒ invariant. Meaningful as-is.
    – SCALING (non-compact, NS-002): E~λ⁻¹, Ω~λ⁺¹, P~λ⁺³ are scale-COVARIANT (GAUGE);
      only H~λ⁰ and E·Ω~λ⁰ are scale-INVARIANT and descend to the quotient.
  • 'SOME SCALES ARE NOT MEANINGFUL' (Aaron) = the scaling-orbit directions: absolute E,Ω,P
    are pure gauge; only scale-invariant combinations carry physics. Scale is a genuine
    NON-COMPACT coordinate you must quotient — and the physical exponents need BOTH the
    field-scaling AND the domain (measure) rescaling (the λ⁻³). Field-scaling alone misses it.
  • SUPERCRITICALITY (NS-002), GEOMETRICALLY: the one Tier-1 coercive control, ENERGY, has
    physical exponent −1 (E~λ⁻¹ → 0 as λ→∞) — gauge at small scales, so it cannot control the
    scale-invariant (critical) quotient where blowup would live. The Step-2 resolution wall is
    the SAME fact in numerical clothing: no finite grid resolves the scale-invariant tail.
  • CFS RHYME (probe, not claim): invariant-coordinate + symmetry-quotient analysis is the
    CFS method; here the invariants are NS's own physical ones. Observe; do not assert transfer.
